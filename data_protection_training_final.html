Foundation
What is the Data Protection Act?
⚖️
Why this matters to you personally
The Act places obligations on individuals who handle personal data — not just on organisations. As a KTB staff member, you have personal legal responsibilities under this law.

The Data Protection Act No. 24 of 2019 is Kenya's primary law governing how personal data is collected, stored, used, and shared. It applies to all organisations — including state corporations like KTB — that handle personal data about individuals.

The Act established the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) with powers to investigate complaints, conduct audits, and impose fines on non-compliant organisations.

Personal data means any information that can identify a living individual:

Standard personal data
Name · ID/passport number · Email & phone · Physical address · Photos & images · Payment details · Travel records
Sensitive data — higher protection
Health & medical records · Race/ethnic origin · Religious beliefs · Biometric data · Criminal records · Immigration status

📌 ScenarioThe Marketing Database

KTB's marketing team has a database of 50,000 travel agents. A colleague wants to use this list for a new unrelated campaign. Is this allowed without further checks?

✗ No — not without verifying consent.
Every person must have consented to receive this type of marketing. Using the list for a different campaign requires confirming that the original consent covers this new use. When in doubt, contact Legal before sending.
3
Core Principles
The 8 Data Protection Principles

Your conduct must reflect these eight principles in everything you do with personal data.

01
Lawfulness & Fairness
Only collect data when you have a legal reason — consent, a contract, or a legal obligation. Not "just in case."
02
Purpose Limitation
Only use data for the purpose it was collected. An event database cannot be used for unrelated marketing.
03
Data Minimisation
Collect only what you actually need. Remove form fields asking for information you will never use.
04
Accuracy
Keep records accurate and up to date. If someone corrects their details, update your records promptly.
05
Storage Limitation
Do not keep personal data longer than necessary. Old spreadsheets and email lists should be regularly purged.
06
Integrity & Confidentiality
Protect data from unauthorised access. Use strong passwords, lock your screen, don't share credentials.
07
Accountability
KTB must demonstrate compliance — keep records of consent, processing activities, and incidents.
08
Data Sovereignty
Data about Kenyans should generally remain in Kenya. Transferring abroad requires specific safeguards and Legal clearance.
4
Your Responsibilities
What you must and must not do
✅ You MUST do these
✓Get proper consent before collecting personal data
✓Tell people why you are collecting their data
✓Use strong, unique passwords for all systems
✓Lock your screen when you leave your desk
✓Report any suspected breach to Legal immediately
✓Delete personal data when no longer needed
✓Share data only with those who need it for their work
❌ You must NEVER do these
✗Share personal data with unauthorised people
✗Send personal data by unencrypted email
✗Share your login credentials with colleagues
✗Store personal data on personal devices or USB drives
✗Use data for purposes other than why it was collected
✗Keep databases without a defined purpose or owner
✗Transfer data outside Kenya without Legal clearance
📌 ScenarioThe Borrowed Login

A trusted colleague urgently needs access to a document and asks to borrow your SharePoint login "just for five minutes." What do you do?

✗ Refuse — and direct them to IT to reset their own password.
Sharing login credentials is always a security breach — no exceptions. Every person must access systems through their own authenticated account so all access to personal data can be audited and traced.

Incidents & Response
What to do if something goes wrong
🚨
The 72-hour rule — a hard legal deadline
KTB must notify the ODPC within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach. This clock starts when anyone at KTB becomes aware — not just Legal. Report immediately. Do not investigate yourself first.
1
STOP
Do not attempt to fix it yourself or cover it up.
2
CONTAIN
If safe to do so — close a file, change a password — act immediately.
3
REPORT — within the hour
Email allan@ktb.go.ke or dmutunga@ktb.go.ke. Do this within the hour, not the next morning.
4
DOCUMENT
Write down what happened, when you noticed, and every action taken. Essential for the ODPC notification.
💡
The golden rule
When in doubt about anything involving personal data — ask Legal before you act. Always better to pause and check than to breach.
