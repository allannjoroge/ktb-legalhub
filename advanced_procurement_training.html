Public Procurement & Asset Disposal Act, 2015

Advanced Practitioners Module — For Procurement, Legal, Audit & Finance Staff

ADVANCED ⏱ 60 min read 📝 30 quiz questions Pass mark: 70% 📅 Act commenced 7 Jan 2016 👥 Procurement · Legal · Audit · Finance
This module has been developed by the Directorate of Legal Services for KTB staff in procurement, legal, audit, and finance who interact with procurement law daily and require in-depth procedural knowledge. The module covers committee roles and segregation of duties, procurement methods and thresholds, the end-to-end tender process, contract management and variations, AGPO reservations, asset disposal, criminal offences, and the administrative review process.

It includes eight sections and a thirty-question quiz. You must score at least 70% (21/30) to pass. This module assumes you have completed the introductory procurement module.
Reviewed and approved by Allan Njoroge, Director of Legal Services & Corporation Secretary, Kenya Tourism Board.
Section 1 of 8
Roles, Committees & Segregation of Duties
The Act requires strict segregation between those who initiate procurement, those who evaluate, those who award, and those who receive goods. No single person should control the entire chain. This protects KTB from fraud and protects you from personal liability.

Accounting Officer (Section 44)

The Accounting Officer (KTB's CEO) bears primary responsibility for KTB's compliance. Key duties include: ensuring procurement is within approved budget (s.44(2)(a)), constituting all procurement committees (s.44(2)(b)), ensuring procurement plans are prepared and submitted (s.44(2)(c)), approving and signing all contracts (s.44(2)(f)), and submitting the AGPO portion of the procurement plan to PPRA within 60 days of the financial year (s.44(2)(i)).

Procurement Function (Section 47, Regulation 33)

Must be staffed by procurement professionals with recognised qualifications. The head of procurement provides professional opinion to the accounting officer (s.47(2), s.84) and acts as secretary to evaluation, inspection, and disposal committees (Reg.33(3)(r)). The professional opinion must accompany every evaluation report submitted to the accounting officer (s.84(1)).

Inspection & Acceptance Committee (Section 48, Regulation 35)

Ad hoc committee appointed by the accounting officer on recommendation of the head of procurement. Must include the user department, technical department, and head of procurement (Reg.35(2)). Inspects goods/works/services immediately after delivery to confirm compliance with contract specifications, issues interim or completion certificates (s.48(4)).

Tender Opening Committee (Section 78, Regulation 25)

At least 3 members; at least one must not be directly involved in processing or evaluation (s.78(1)(b)). Opens all tenders immediately after the deadline. Each member must sign each tender on determined pages and initial against quoted prices (s.78(9)).

🔍 Practitioner Scenario
The head of procurement at KTB is appointed as secretary to the evaluation committee for a KES 20 million media buying tender. During evaluation, a committee member starts discussing their individual scores before completing their own independent assessment. The head of procurement says nothing. What are the issues?
Two issues: (1) Regulation 31(1) requires each member to evaluate independently before sharing analysis, questions, evaluation, or ratings. Sharing scores prematurely breaches this regulation and could invalidate the evaluation. (2) The head of procurement, as secretary, has a duty to ensure compliance (Reg.33(3)(a)) and should have intervened to stop the premature discussion and recorded the deviation. Failure to intervene may constitute a personal accountability issue under Section 45(5).

🔍 Practitioner Scenario
KTB needs to procure branded promotional merchandise (bags, caps, lanyards) for a tourism expo. The total budget is KES 4.2 million. The procurement officer proposes using RFQ. Is this correct?
No. RFQ for goods is capped at KES 3 million per the Second Schedule. At KES 4.2 million, this procurement must use open tender (the default method) or restricted tender under s.102(1)(b) if the conditions are met (max KES 30M for goods). Splitting this into two RFQs of KES 2.1 million each would be contract splitting under s.54(1) — a criminal offence.

Tender Security (Section 61)

Max 2% of tender value (s.61(2)(c)). Stated as absolute value. Forfeited if bidder withdraws after deadline or refuses to sign contract (s.61(3)). Not required for AGPO categories — they use a Tender Securing Declaration Form instead (s.61(5), Reg.155).

Tender Validity Extension (Section 88)

Maximum 30 days, once only (s.88(3)). Notice in writing to each bidder (s.88(2)). Security forfeited if bidder withdraws after accepting extension (s.88(4)).

Section 4 of 8
Contracts, Variations & Performance Security

Contract Formation (Section 135)

No contract exists until the written contract is signed by both parties (s.135(4)). The 14-day standstill period must elapse after notification before signing (s.135(3)). The tender documents form the basis of all contracts and must include at minimum: Contract Agreement Form, Tender Form, price schedule/BOQ, Schedule of Requirements, Technical Specs, General Conditions, Special Conditions, Notification of Award (s.135(6)).

Advance Payment (Sections 146–148)

No payment before delivery/acceptance except where specified in tender documents and contract (s.146). Maximum 20% of tender price (s.147(1)). Requires advance payment security equivalent to the advance itself. Must be used only for the contract — misuse makes the advance an immediately recoverable debt (s.148).

Sub-contracting (Section 149)

Permitted unless tender documents prohibit it. Sub-contractor must not be debarred and must not have participated in the related procurement. The main contractor remains responsible for the sub-contractor's obligations (s.149(2)).

🔍 Practitioner Scenario
A contractor awarded a KES 12 million goods supply contract signed 8 months ago requests a 20% price increase due to currency fluctuations. The user department supports the request. Can the head of procurement approve this?
No — on multiple grounds. (1) Section 139(3) prohibits any upward price variation within the first 12 months. The contract is only 8 months old. (2) Even after 12 months, any price variation must be based on the prevailing CPI from KNBS (s.139(4)(a)), not currency fluctuations. (3) The head of procurement does not approve variations — variations require approval by the accounting officer on the recommendation of an evaluation committee (s.139(1)(a), s.139(2)). The contractor must wait until after 12 months and demonstrate CPI-linked justification.

AGPO — Preferences & Reservations

The 30% Mandate (Sections 53(6), 157(5), 157(10))

Every procuring entity must allocate at least 30% of its annual procurement budget to youth, women, and persons with disability. This is not discretionary — it is a constitutional obligation under Articles 55, 56, and 227(2).

Exclusive Preference Thresholds (Regulation 163)

Exclusive preference for citizen contractors: up to KES 1 billion for works/construction materials; up to KES 500 million for goods and services. Above these thresholds, international competition is permitted but with margins of preference (Reg.164).

Section 6 of 8
Asset Disposal & Inventory Management

Disposal Committee (Section 163)

Established by the accounting officer as and when needed. Responsible for verification and processing of all disposal recommendations in liaison with the head of procurement (s.163(2)).

Disposal Process (Section 164)

StepWhoWhat
1Employee in chargeBrings unserviceable/obsolete/surplus assets to disposal committee's attention through the head of procurement, within a reasonable time (s.164(1)–(2))
2Technical expertPrepares technical report, sets reserve price (minimum acceptable price below real market value) (s.164(3))
3Disposal committeeMeets within prescribed period, conducts survey, reviews items and technical report, recommends best disposal method to AO (s.164(4))
4AODiscloses reserve price to prospective buyers (s.164(5)). May approve or reject committee recommendation (s.164(7)). If rejected, gives further direction (s.164(9)).
5AOIf no responsive bidder at reserve price, AO may revise the reserve price on disposal committee recommendation (s.164(6))

Methods of Disposal (Section 165)

Transfer to another public entity (with or without financial adjustment), sale by public tender, sale by public auction, trade-in, waste disposal management, or as prescribed. Radioactive/electronic waste must be disposed only to persons licensed under EMCA 1999 (s.165(2)).

Section 166 — Restriction on disposal to employees: KTB cannot dispose of assets to its own employees or board/committee members except as expressly allowed under the Act and regulations. An AO who disposes assets to employees through artificial valuation commits an offence (s.166(2)).

Inventory & Stores Management (Sections 159–162)

Head of procurement must arrange quarterly inspection visits and conduct quarterly and annual inventory and stock-taking (s.162(2)). All procured items must be taken on charge before use (s.162(1)). Overstocking must be avoided (s.162(3)). The Authority issues manuals and guidelines on inventory management (s.162(6)).

Penalties (Sections 176(2)–(3), 177)

Individuals

  • Fine up to KES 4 million
  • Imprisonment up to 10 years
  • Or both
  • Plus internal disciplinary action
  • Plus personal reimbursement for losses caused (s.54(4), s.66(7))

Body Corporate

  • Fine up to KES 10 million
  • Plus debarment of non-public officers
  • Contract voidable at procuring entity's option (s.176(4)(b))

Debarment (Section 41)

Minimum 3 years. Grounds include: offence under the Act, breach of contract including poor performance, false information, refusal to enter contract, breach of code of ethics, tax default, corrupt/fraudulent practices, violation of employment laws, filing frivolous review requests. Spouse, child, or sub-contractor's involvement can trigger debarment of the principal (s.55(2)).

Confidentiality Breach (Section 67(5))

Debarred + prohibited from government work for 10 years. Any person who discloses evaluation information, bid contents, or confidential procurement data faces debarment and a 10-year prohibition from working for any government entity or entity where government holds shares.

Good Faith Protection (Section 178)

You are protected from personal liability for acts done in good faith in performance of duties. KTB will indemnify you if you are sued for official actions — provided you were not guilty of gross negligence, misconduct, or grave abuse of office, and you engaged external counsel.

Review Board Powers (Section 173)

The Review Board can: annul any part or all of the procurement proceedings (a); give directions to the AO (b); substitute its own decision for the AO's (c); order costs between parties (d); order termination and fresh procurement (e).
